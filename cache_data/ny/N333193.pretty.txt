RE:  The tariff classification of Milk Protein Concentrates
from Ireland
Dear Ms. Strother:
In your letter dated May 30, 2023, you requested a tariff
classification ruling on behalf of your client, Glanbia Ireland DAC (Kilkenny,
Ireland). Samples were submitted with your ruling request and were forwarded to
the Customs and Border Protection Laboratory for analysis. This analysis has
been completed. We apologize for the delay.
The subject merchandise consists of the following milk protein
concentrate products - Solmiko MPC 1001 (81.1 percent milk protein), Solmiko
MPC TGF 1004 (83.6 percent milk protein), and Solmiko HD MPC powder (84.5
percent milk protein). Each product is produced from one common ingredient
which is 100 percent skimmed cow’s milk that has undergone
ultra-filteration, concentration and subsequently spray dried into a powder.
The products will be used to manufacture adult nutrition products. Additionally,
the food applications for these products will include the manufacture of
ready-to-mix beverages, sports nutrition bars, and ready-to-drink products.
Solmiko MPC 1001 and Solmiko MPC TGF 1004 will be packed in sacks with a
net weight of 20 kilograms.  Solmiko HD MPC will be packed in sacks with a
net weight of 550 kilograms.
The applicable subheading for the Milk Protein Concentrates will
be 0404.90.1000, Harmonized Tariff Schedule of the United States (HTSUS), which
provides for: “Whey, whether or not concentrated or containing added
sugar or other sweetening matter; products consisting of natural milk
constituents, whether or not containing added sugar or other sweetening matter,
not elsewhere specified or included: Other: Milk Protein Concentrates.”
The rate of duty will be 0.37 cents per kilogram.
Duty rates are provided for your convenience and are subject to
change.  The text of the most recent HTSUS and the accompanying duty rates
are provided on World Wide Web at
https://hts.usitc.gov/current
.
